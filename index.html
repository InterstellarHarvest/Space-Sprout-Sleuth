SAA — AGENT HANDBOOK

THE INVESTIGATION LOOP

Every case follows the same rhythm. You'll observe the scene, question your four sources, gather clues, and then diagnose — backing your call with evidence.

TALK TO CREWInterview the station crew.
REVIEW LOGSRead mission records.
EXAMINE PLANTSInspect the crops directly.
CHECK SENSORSScan environmental readings.

All four sources are available from the start of every case. You can revisit any of them as often as you like.

PERSONALITIES & MOOD

The crew you meet aren't interchangeable NPCs. Each has a personality — patient, prickly, stoic, stressed, and more — that shapes how they respond to your questions.

ASTRONAUT KIM Patient. Will tolerate repeated questions, but keep things on-topic and she'll share more. Other crew members won't be so forgiving.

Push too hard, repeat yourself, or ask rudely and their mood drops. A locked-out crew member can cut off entire lines of evidence. Some personalities forgive; some don't. Read the room.

CLUES & DIAGNOSIS

Clues you've uncovered are logged to your case file and unlock deeper questions. The Diagnose button lights up once you have enough evidence to reasonably call it — but you can press early. Guessing without clues still works; you just miss bonus insight and risk a rank hit on a wrong call.

RANK & PROGRESSION

Solve cases well to climb the SAA career ladder. Sloppy diagnoses cost rank; thorough, evidence-backed ones earn it.
